# LinKod Trademark Policy
### Trademark Owner: Eric Rush (Sebastian)

This Trademark Policy explains how the name “LinKod,” its associated marks,
and branding elements may be used. The purpose of this policy is to protect
the identity, integrity, and reputation of the LinKod programming language
as it grows and welcomes contributors.

The LinKod trademark is owned exclusively by:

**Eric Rush (Sebastian)**  
Creator & Architect of the LinKod Language.

---

## 1. What This Policy Covers

This policy applies to all uses of:

- the name “LinKod”
- the phrase “LinKod Language”
- the phrase “LinKod Runtime”
- the phrase “LinKod VM”
- the phrase “LinKod Pro”
- the phrase “LinKod Enterprise”
- the LinKod glyph emblem (if present)
- any official LinKod logos or symbols
- any branding associated with the LinKod project

These names and marks represent the official identity of the LinKod
ecosystem and may not be used without permission.

---

## 2. Allowed Uses (No Permission Required)

You may use the name “LinKod” in the following ways:

- To refer to the official LinKod project
- To describe compatibility (e.g., “works with LinKod”)
- To discuss LinKod in articles, videos, tutorials, or documentation
- To link to the official repository
- To reference LinKod in academic or educational material
- To describe your own project as “inspired by LinKod”

These uses are allowed as long as they do not imply official status,
endorsement, or affiliation.

---

## 3. Uses That Require Permission

You must obtain written permission from **Eric Rush (Sebastian)** before
using the LinKod trademark in any of the following ways:

- Naming a product, fork, or project “LinKod”
- Using the name “LinKod” in a company or organization name
- Creating a “LinKod Pro,” “LinKod Enterprise,” or similar product
- Using LinKod branding on merchandise
- Using LinKod branding in marketing or advertising
- Creating an official-looking logo or glyph for a fork
- Distributing modified versions under the LinKod name

Permission may be granted or denied at the sole discretion of the trademark
owner.

---

## 4. Forbidden Uses (Permission Will Not Be Granted)

The following uses are strictly prohibited:

- Claiming your fork is the official LinKod language
- Using the LinKod name for competing runtimes or engines
- Selling commercial products under the LinKod name
- Using LinKod branding to mislead users
- Creating unofficial “LinKod Pro” or “LinKod Enterprise” versions
- Using the LinKod name in ways that damage the project’s reputation
- Using the LinKod name to imply governance authority

These restrictions protect the identity and integrity of the language.

---

## 5. Forking Rules

LinKod is open-source under the Apache 2.0 License. You may fork the code,
but:

- You **must rename** your fork
- You **may not** use the LinKod trademark
- You **may not** claim your fork is official
- You **may not** use LinKod branding or logos
- You **may not** use “LinKod” in your project name

Examples of acceptable fork names:

- “StoneScript”
- “GlyphLang”
- “FlowCode”
- “Sebastian-Fork-LK” (if clearly unofficial)

Forking is allowed — brand confusion is not.

---

## 7. Community Projects

Community projects may use the LinKod name only in descriptive ways, such as:

- “LinKod Tools”
- “LinKod Extensions”
- “LinKod Tutorials”
- “LinKod Documentation Helpers”

These names must:

- clearly indicate unofficial status
- not imply endorsement
- not imply governance authority
- not use official logos or glyphs

If a name could confuse users, it is not allowed.

---

## 8. Logo and Glyph Usage

If LinKod has an official logo or glyph emblem:

- It may not be used on forks
- It may not be used on merchandise
- It may not be used in commercial products
- It may not be used in branding without permission
- It may not be altered or remixed to imply official status

The glyph emblem is part of LinKod’s symbolic identity and must be protected.

---

## 9. Enforcement

Violations of this Trademark Policy may result in:

- written warnings
- requests to rename projects
- removal of branding
- revocation of contribution rights
- public clarification of unofficial status
- legal action in severe cases

Enforcement is at the sole discretion of the trademark owner.
