language specification, symbolic system, glyph architecture, ritual

programming language.

The LinKod specification and all associated symbolic constructs are owned
exclusively by:

**Eric Rush (Sebastian)**  
Creator & Architect of the LinKod Language.

This ownership applies to all parts of the LinKod language, including:

### **1.1. The Language Specification**
- Syntax rules  
- Structural grammar

- Elements (Flow, Flame, Dusk, Stone)

- Symbolic modifiers

- Glyph behavior rules

### **1.4. Ritual & Seal Logic**
- Ritual invocation rules  
- Seal verification structure  
- Authentication semantics  
- Security symbolism  

### **1.5. Conceptual & Mythic Framework**
- Cosmology  
- Philosophy  
- Identity rules  
- Symbolic hierarchy  

These components define the LinKod language and may not be altered without
explicit approval from the owner.

## 2. Authority Over the Specification

All decisions regarding the LinKod specification are made exclusively by:

**Eric Rush (Sebastian)**  
Creator, Architect, and Final Authority.

This includes:

- approving or rejecting changes  
- defining new glyphs  
- modifying symbolic rules  
- updating ritual structure  
- adjusting Seal logic  
- evolving the language philosophy  
- determining breaking changes  
- maintaining long-term direction  

No contributor or external party may claim authority over the specification.

---

## 3. Contributor Rights and Limitations

Contributors may:

- propose changes  
- suggest glyphs  
- create modules  
- write documentation  
- assist with runtime development  

Contributors may **not**:

- modify the official specification directly  
- redefine glyph meanings  
- alter ritual or Seal logic  
- change Element or Season behavior  
- publish an “official” spec  
- claim ownership of any part of the language  
- create competing specifications under the LinKod name  

All contributions are subject to review and approval by the owner.

---

## 4. Forking Rules

LinKod is open-source under the Apache 2.0 License. Forks are allowed, but:

- forks must use a different name  
- forks may not use LinKod branding  
- forks may not use LinKod glyphs or symbols  
- forks may not use LinKod’s Seal or ritual structure  
- forks may not claim to be official  
- forks may not publish a “LinKod Specification”  

Forks may create their own specification, but it must be clearly distinct
and not derived from LinKod’s symbolic identity.
